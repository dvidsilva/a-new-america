WHAT WE DO

AnewAmerica CDFI complements our sister organization AnewAmerica Community Corporation by providing financial products and services to low income entrepreneurs residing in local, low income San Francisco Bay Area communities. We provide a continuum of financial services to complement traditional business incubation services offered by AnewAmerica Community Corporation thereby filling the need that low income microenterprises have for financial capital to start, or grow their business.

ABOUT US

AnewAmerica CDFI (ANA CDFI) is a 501c3 non-profit certified by the US Department of the Treasury as a Community Development Financial Institution (CDFI ). ANA CDFI provides debt financing and business advisory assistance to low income microenterprise entrepreneurs. Our financing capabilities combined with business advisory assistance benefits low income micro entrepreneurs by providing them with the capital they need to not only grow their microenterprises but to also improve their families household incomes.

ANA CDFI was created to complement its sister agency AnewAmerica Community Corporation and that agency’s existing microenterprise business development services for low income entrepreneurs that includes: financial education, asset building, business training, and one-on-one technical assistance programs.

The two agencies, AnewAmerica Community Corporation and AnA CDFI, working in tandem provide the full spectrum of business development support services and financial capital needed to successfully launch and expand microenterprises. Our joint goals are to (1) increase self-sufficiency of low-income families by expanding microenterprises, thereby creating sustainable jobs and (2) to assist low-income individuals to build financial assets for long-term sustainability. We are committed to increasing economic security and upward mobility for those living and working in low-income communities in the Bay Area.

WHAT IS A CDFI?

  • A certified Community Development Financial Institution (CDFI) is a specialized financial institution that works in market niches that are underserved by traditional financial institutions.
  • CDFIs are certified by the Community Development Financial Institutions Fund (CDFI Fund) at the U.S. Department of the Treasury.
  • All CDFIs share a common mission of community development though increasing economic stability by financing businesses, creating jobs, and rebuilding neighborhoods.
  • There are six basic types of CDFIs: loan funds, banks, credit unions, microenterprise funds, corporation-based lenders and investors, and venture funds.
  • Certified CDFIs can be funded from a number of sources, including the federal government, state and local governments, religious institutions, private foundations, as well as individuals.
